LLM concepts I - Tokenizers

01 Jul 2024
AI

To understand how LLMs work, you have to understand embeddings. And to understand embeddings, you have to understand tokenizers.

LLMs receive text as input and generate text as output, but as neural networks, they work with numbers, so they first need to convert the text to numbers and then convert the resulting numbers from their calculations into words again.

Converting from text (or other types of data like audio or images) to numbers is called embedding, and we’ll discuss it in a future post. For now, it is enough to know that an embedding is a vector representing a part of the text: a sentence, a word, or a part of a word, depending on the specific type of embedding used. This “part of a text” is called a token, and the process of splitting a text into tokens is called tokenization.
